About the Artist

Striking hot iron since 2012.

The Man at the Anvil

I believe that metal has a memory. Every hammer strike, every heat cycle, leaves an imprint not just on the shape, but on the soul of the piece. My work is an exploration of this dialogue between force and flow.

Trained in traditional joinery and tool-making, I now focus on blending these ancient methods with organic, expressive forms. Whether it's a kitchen knife meant to be an heirloom or a gate that welcomes you home, each object is forged with intent.

Skills & Focus

Beyond the Forge

The person behind the hammer

When I'm not at the forge, you'll find me exploring the forests that inspire my organic designs, or teaching workshops to pass on traditional techniques to the next generation of blacksmiths.

My family has been in metalwork for three generations, but I'm the first to focus on artistic expression rather than purely functional pieces. This blend of heritage and innovation defines my approach to the craft.

I believe that in our fast-paced, mass-produced world, there's something deeply meaningful about objects made by hand, with care, and meant to last generations. Every piece I create is a rebellion against disposability.
